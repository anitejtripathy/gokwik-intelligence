The Model

Channel-as-a-Service: What It Means

The modern growth agency model does not produce one-off ads. It manufactures and manages entire digital ecosystems on behalf of brands. The agency owns the full content lifecycle: strategy, scripting, editing, publishing, community management, and analytics. For a brand, their YouTube channel and Instagram feed become productised, recurring-value assets — not campaigns. This is “Channel-as-a-Service.” The archetype agency for this model is MarkitUp (Bengaluru, est. 2021, 51–100 employees).

Who runs GrowKwik: The channel is co-built by what the industry calls the “video mafia” — a network of content production groups operating multiple channels across different verticals simultaneously. GrowKwik is believed to be managed by the AEOS group, which offers “YAAS” (YouTube-as-a-Service): they take complete ownership of a brand’s channel — finding content angles, scripting, filming, editing, publishing, and managing comments. Vargab Bakshi’s face (and possibly an AI avatar of him) is used as the brand identity for GrowKwik, lending it a credible human voice without requiring his active involvement in individual videos.
How Editors Are Manufactured

The Content Engine & Editor Training System

Growth agencies train editors from scratch in a standardised system. This enables scale without quality loss. The training covers four domains:

✂️
Retention Editing
Cinematic cuts, dramatic music, organised B-roll to maximise watch-time. Goal: beat the platform’s retention metrics, which are the primary driver of organic reach.
🧠
Psychological Hooking
Thumbnails and titles that trigger curiosity and high click-through rates. The “front door” of the ecosystem — no conversion happens without the click.
📊
Algorithmic Optimisation
Platform-specific technical requirements for YouTube Shorts, long-form, and Instagram Reels. Maximise organic reach without paid distribution.
💰
Sales Integration
Editors trained to ensure every video ends with a measurable CTA tied to the client’s conversion funnel — not just views.
Education-as-a-Service

GoKwik’s Core Strategic Innovation

GrowKwik operates as an “Education-as-a-Service” platform. By providing free, high-value education to D2C entrepreneurs on checkout optimisation, COD management, and growth strategy, GoKwik establishes itself as an industry authority before any direct pitch is made. The market knows GoKwik’s value before a sales team ever engages. GoKwik’s 130 million shoppers and 12,000+ brands (Lenskart, Neeman’s, Shoppers Stop) provide a proprietary data flywheel: real merchant insights that become content, which drives more merchant acquisitions, which deepens the data.

A GTM Lesson for Razorpay

Why This Is a Superior GTM Motion

GrowKwik is, at its core, a highly effective go-to-market channel. It generates warm, self-qualified leads at near-zero marginal cost and builds category understanding before the sales cycle begins. This contrasts sharply with how we at Magic Checkout have handled GTM for our own product improvements. For example, the shipping engine — a feature that directly improves Magic’s reliability via shipping uptime and reduces checkout latency — took over 9 months to reach 80%+ merchant adoption. A content engine targeting D2C founders at the awareness stage would have made such adoption dramatically faster. GoKwik’s model shows that the brand that educates the merchant base owns the adoption curve.
