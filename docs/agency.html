The Model · Channel-as-a-Service

What “Channel-as-a-Service” Actually Means

The modern growth agency model — exemplified by MarkitUp (Bengaluru, est. 2021 by Ishan Sharma & Saransh Anand, 51–100 employees) — does not produce one-off ads. It manufactures and manages entire digital ecosystems on behalf of brands. The agency owns the full content lifecycle: strategy, scripting, editing, publishing, community management, and analytics. For a brand, this means their YouTube channel, Instagram Reels feed, and LinkedIn presence become productized, recurring-value assets — not marketing campaigns. This is “Channel-as-a-Service.”

GoKwik has built this internally. GrowKwik is GoKwik’s own Channel-as-a-Service — led by CGO Vargab Bakshi (formerly Head of Partnerships at Shopify India, APAC ecosystem lead at Wix) and operating across YouTube and Instagram with 500,000+ cumulative followers.

How Editors Are Manufactured

The Content Engine & Editor Training Model

Growth agencies do not just hire editors — they train them from scratch in a standardised system. This is what enables scale without quality loss. The training covers four specialised domains:

✂️
Retention Editing
Cinematic cuts, dramatic music, organised B-roll to maximise watch-time. Goal: beat the platform’s retention metrics, which are the primary driver of organic algorithmic reach.
🧠
Psychological Hooking
Developing thumbnails and titles that trigger curiosity and high click-through rates. Considered the “front door” of the digital ecosystem — no conversion happens without the click.
📊
Algorithmic Optimisation
Understanding technical requirements of each platform — YouTube long-form vs. Instagram Reels vs. Shorts — to maximise organic reach without paid distribution costs.
💰
Sales Integration
Training editors to ensure content drives viewers toward a specific business goal — not just views. Every video ends with a measurable CTA tied to the client’s conversion funnel.
The key insight: By standardising the “secret skill” behind engaging content, the agency scales operations without quality loss. A brand’s channel feels authentic to the creator’s voice while meeting professional media production standards.

Education-as-a-Service

GoKwik’s Core Strategic Innovation

GrowKwik operates as a “Education-as-a-Service” platform. By providing free, high-value education to D2C entrepreneurs on marketing, checkout optimisation, COD management, and growth strategy, GoKwik establishes itself as an industry authority before any direct pitch is made. This creates brand recognition at the problem-awareness stage — the market knows GoKwik’s value before a sales team ever engages.

GoKwik’s 130 million shoppers and 12,000+ brands (including Lenskart, Neeman’s, and Shoppers Stop) provide a rich data flywheel — real merchant insights that become GrowKwik content, which drives more merchant acquisitions, which deepens the data flywheel. This loop is extremely difficult to replicate without the underlying merchant network.

Agency Case Studies

How the Model Works for Other Brands

These case studies from MarkitUp — the archetype agency for this model — demonstrate what content-led growth agency work achieves in practice.

Luru · ProductHunt Launch & Technical Communication

Luru (sales technology / GTM platform) partnered with MarkitUp to facilitate a ProductHunt launch. The agency produced a high-quality video using custom-made screen grabs and creative animations to visually simplify complex features.

Key outcomes: Technical products suffer from a communication gap — the agency’s animation-led approach allowed Luru to explain complex functionality in an emotionally engaging way. The professional content quality contributed to a successful launch. The agency worked directly with Luru’s Head of GTM & Sales to align content with broader sales goals — not just views.

Source: MarkitUp portfolio case study · B2B SaaS application of the model

Vauld × Alfa Romeo · Cross-Brand Prestige Production

Vauld (crypto platform) partnered with MarkitUp to produce a promotional ad celebrating a partnership with Alfa Romeo. The edit used alluring text overlays and high-quality effects to match Alfa Romeo’s prestige brand identity.

Key outcomes: The agency successfully balanced two distinct brand identities — a crypto platform and a luxury automotive brand — in a single cohesive piece. This demonstrates that growth agencies can handle complex brand guidelines and multi-party collaborations, not just founder storytelling. The production quality elevated the perceived legitimacy of the Vauld brand through association.

Source: MarkitUp portfolio case study · Brand association application of the model

The Fear Playbook

Category Manufacturing Through Crisis Narratives

Fear Hook

Positions GoKwik as the emergency replacement for Shopify’s native COD tool. Targets founders currently using Shopify for COD — the exact segment GoKwik wants to convert. Creates displacement urgency that would not exist without this manufactured narrative.
